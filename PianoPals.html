The Tacy Foundation

Piano Pals in Schools

Piano Pals is a program run in partnership with schools that provides elementary school students who cannot afford piano lessons an opportunity to begin piano study with a middle school or high school mentor. Middle and high school students earn SSL credit for their service. School staff (music teacher or counselor) identify students who would be eligible in terms of need (children who receive free and reduced meals) and potential interest. Students from any grade can be recommended. The school provides the room and the piano/keyboard. The Tacy Foundation provides the mentors and supplies the music curriculum books. Piano Pals is currently running in several schools in Montgomery County, MD.

Piano Pals at the National Military Medical Center for Military Families

Piano Pals is also running at the National Military Medical Center in Bethesda, MD. Any child of a military member receiving treatment at the Medical Center is eligible for the program. If you would like to get more information or enroll your child, please fill out this contact form.

Guitar Pals

Guitar Pals offers an afterschool mentoring activity for middle school students who cannot afford guitar lessons. High school guitar students provide mentoring. The Foundation supplies music books.
